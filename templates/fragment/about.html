About SINDy

Suppose we have a set of measurements \( x(t) \in \mathbb{R}^n \) from some physical system at different points in time \( t \). SINDy seeks to represent the time evolution of \( x(t) \) in terms of a nonlinear function \( f \):

\[ \frac{d}{dt}x(t) = f(x(t)) \]

The key idea behind SINDy is that \( f \) is often sparse in the space of an appropriate set of basis functions — meaning only a small number of terms are nonzero.

For this module, we use Python's pySINDy package to implement SINDy.

The Approximation Problem

Data are aggregated into matrices \( X \) and \( \dot{X} \), then a library matrix \( \Theta(X) \) is formed whose columns consist of candidate basis functions:

\[ X = \begin{bmatrix} x_1(t_1) & \dots & x_n(t_1) \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_1(t_m) & \dots & x_n(t_m) \end{bmatrix}, \quad \dot{X} = \begin{bmatrix} \dot{x}_1(t_1) & \dots & \dot{x}_n(t_1) \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \dot{x}_1(t_m) & \dots & \dot{x}_n(t_m) \end{bmatrix} \]
\[ \Theta(X) = \begin{bmatrix} | & | & & | \\ \theta_1(X) & \theta_2(X) & \dots & \theta_\ell(X) \\ | & | & & | \end{bmatrix} \]

The Core SINDy Problem

SINDy seeks a set of sparse coefficient vectors collected into a matrix \( \Xi \), solving:

\[ \dot{X} \approx \Theta(X)\Xi \]

Each column \( \xi_i \) of \( \Xi \) represents the coefficients for the \( i \)-th component of the model. SINDy uses sparse regression to ensure the resulting model is both accurate and parsimonious — using the simplest possible form.

For example, a sparse \( f(x) \) might look like:

\[ \frac{d}{dt}x = \begin{bmatrix} 1 - x_1 + 3x_1x_2 \\ x_2^2 - 5x_1^3 \end{bmatrix} \]
