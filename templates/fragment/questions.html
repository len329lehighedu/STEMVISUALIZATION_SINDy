General Questions & User Guide

Find answers to common questions about the SINDy Identification Module and how to interpret the results.

How do I start the training process?

Navigate to the Interactive Module tab. In the "Train" sub-tab, select your desired dataset from the dropdown menu, adjust the Sparsity Threshold, and click the Run Train button. The system will process the data and update the Leaderboard below.

What does the "Sparsity Threshold" (λ) represent?

The Sparsity Threshold is the core parameter of the SINDy algorithm. It determines the "aggressiveness" of the feature selection:

  • Low Threshold: The model keeps more terms, potentially leading to higher accuracy but a more complex (and less physical) equation.
  • High Threshold: The model forces more coefficients to zero, favoring simpler, more interpretable physical laws.
How can I compare different models?

Every time you run a training session, the results are saved in the History Leaderboard. You can compare models based on their MSE (Mean Squared Error) and the number of active terms. To test a specific model, go to the Test or Predict tabs and select the Model ID from the dropdown.

Why is the prediction diverging after some time?

SINDy models are sensitive to the Prediction Horizon. If the identified dynamics are slightly unstable or if the data was noisy, errors can accumulate during integration. Try increasing the Sparsity Threshold to find a more robust physical representation.
