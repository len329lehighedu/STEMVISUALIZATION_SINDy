General Questions

Understand your model deeper

How to compare the models?

Every time you run a training session, the results are saved in the History Leaderboard. You can compare models based on the metrics (\( R^2 \), RMSE, MAE), residual statistics, and the number of active terms. To test a specific model, go to the Test or Predict tabs and select the Model ID from the dropdown.

What's the difference between the metrics shown in the Training History table and the metrics shown in the Test tab?

Training/Validation metrics (\( R^2 \), RMSE, MAE) are computed on the derivative space (\( dX/dt \)) — this is what SINDy's STLSQ optimizer directly fits. Test tab metrics are computed on the state space (\( x(t) \)), after forward-simulating the discovered equations with solve_ivp. These two sets of numbers use different units and are not directly comparable. A model can have excellent Training RMSE(\( dX \)) and still show poor Test RMSE(\( x \)) if the small derivative-space errors compound during simulation. Always check both.

Why is the prediction diverging after some time?

SINDy models are sensitive to the Prediction Horizon. If the identified dynamics are slightly unstable or if the data was noisy, errors can accumulate during integration. Try increasing the Sparsity Threshold to find a more robust physical representation.

Why is R² high but the simulated trajectory still diverges from the true data?

\( R^2 \) shown in the Training tab is computed on the derivative space (\( dX/dt \)), which is what SINDy's loss function actually optimizes — not on the simulated trajectory \( x(t) \). A model can fit the derivative field almost perfectly and still diverge when numerically integrated, because small coefficient errors compound over time during simulation. This is exactly why the Test tab exists: it forward-integrates your equations with solve_ivp and reports RMSE/\( R^2 \) on \( x(t) \) directly, which is the metric that actually matters for prediction.

What does "Sparsity parameter is too big and eliminated all coefficients" mean?

Your Sparsity Threshold is set higher than the magnitude of every coefficient in the true (or best-fit) equations, so STLSQ pruned everything to zero — the model effectively found \( dX/dt = 0 \) for your system. Lower the threshold (try the "type exact threshold" box for fine control) and re-train.
