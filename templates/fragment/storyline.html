Discover the equations hidden in your data

Ever wish there was a faster way to find the mathematical laws governing your experimental data?

The problem: you have raw measurements, but no known differential equation describing how the system evolves.

Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) searches a library of candidate functions and keeps only the sparsest, cleanest combination that explains your data — turning a CSV file into a governing ODE.

How it works

Interpretable

Output is a real equation, not a black-box weight matrix — every term has a physical meaning.

Data-driven

No prior knowledge of the underlying physics required — only measurements of the system's states.

Sparse

Thresholded regression prunes weak terms, favoring the simplest equation that still fits.
